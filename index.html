Combinatorics at the Confluence

An ICM Satellite Conference

July 20–22, 2026 Pittsburgh, PA

About

Combinatorics at the Confluence will bring together researchers from across the diverse landscape of combinatorics, including algebraic, enumerative, extremal, geometric, probabilistic, and topological combinatorics. The meeting will feature plenary talks, three parallel special sessions, an ICARM tutorial, and a poster reception.

There will be featured talks and events by the Institute for Computer-Aided Reasoning in Mathematics.

We gratefully acknowledge support from the National Science Foundation.

From Pittsburgh International Airport: 28X Airport Flyer

Pittsburgh Regional Transit’s 28X Airport Flyer runs directly from Pittsburgh International Airport to Oakland and Carnegie Mellon campus. At the airport, follow signs to ground transportation and board outside Door 9 on the Ground Level.

The fare is $2.75, paid when boarding. Exact cash, ConnectCard/ConnecTix, and mobile tickets are accepted. Use the PRT Ready2Ride app. Credit and debit cards are not accepted on the bus; a fare vending machine that accepts cards is near Door 8.

Hilton Garden Inn University Place

Get off at Forbes Avenue + McKee Place (PRT stop 28), immediately beside the hotel.

Carnegie Mellon campus

Get off at Morewood Avenue at Forbes Avenue, far side (PRT stop 22783), just after the bus turns onto Morewood Avenue.

The inbound 28X is discharge-only after reaching Oakland, so it should not be used for travel from the Hilton Garden Inn to Carnegie Mellon.
